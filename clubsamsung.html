Re/imagining - Club Samsung

Samsung's Entertainment App

Role: Lead Designer Team: Designers | Ux Researcher | Product Manager Client: Samsung Device: Mobile & Smart TV (Android App) Duration: 2014 - 2015

Project Overview
Context

Club Samsung launched in 2014, it serves as a one stop entertainment hub for Samsung India audience serving up content such as Live Tv, Movies, Music & Live Events. It's USP is to give users Bollywood & Regional entertainment on the go. I was a part of the previous design team, where I had worked on the research, Interaction design and visual design of the Mobile, Tablet and TV App.

Planned was re-design Club Samsung's Entertainment app for Samsung store solved many problems that had manifested over the years. It aimed to modernize music app by bringing new features which makes more interesting and connecting to the music lovers. It is also helped influence its future direction.


Problem Statement

Our research team identified 4 primary problems with the first version of the app.

1. Performance
App was slow to load when compared to other apps, and user were bouncing. They expected the app to be as fast as mobile, if not faster.

3. Connected
The app has limited social connect options to share what I am listening with the peers, and to try out the songs they are listening to.
2. Interactive
The app does a good job playing its playlists but it does not give me interactive playful experinece to discove new music.


4. Cluttered
User felt there are too many features and content and the app feels bloated at times. Though the visuals were edgy user feels a little too big and as a result make screen looks heavy.

Goal
We converted our key problems into opportunities to solve for during the redesign.

1.Performance (Slow → Fast)
The new app should have competitively fast performance by improving Time-to-Interaction and increasing the overall perceived interface responsiveness.

3.Connected
Enable users to quick interact with each other during the music sharing experience. Also integrate this feature with the existing application and making it work within a users' current listening habits
2.Interactive
The user interface of the app should be visually refreshed, which is based on modern App tech

4. Cluttered → Relevant
The redesign should make key features easier to find and use, remove underperforming features, and ensure we show the right content to the user across app.
Process
Understand the user

I started the project with 4 above goal: to see how people are currently sharing music with each other and I needed to make sure the new feature would integrate seamlessly to the Club Samsung could make that process better. Since I didn't yet have a problem to solve for, I then conducted some competitor analysis to see what features they had and if they had any similar features. I also wanted to get a sense of their UX and see how we could do better. I created a research plan and interview guide and found 4 different types of music listeners to interview to find out:

  • - What sharing music means to user
  • - How, why, and when they share music
  • - What frustrates them about the process currently
  • - Listeners are open to others’ options in terms of making playlists.
  • - Most people are satisfied with music collaborations as both listener and contributor.
  • - Most people choose music based on their current mood. (fitness, walking, yoga, relaxing, etc)

How does the user discover new music?

The primary goal of the user is to discover new music - however can happen in myriad ways. Here are some insights from desktop research and interview with users close to the persona.

From Social Circle
- User give credence to music recommendations received from friends & family with whom they share similar tastes.
- Recommendation could be recieved online - shared via a music service, tagged on social media site or sent a direct messag on instant messaging.

Through Algorithms
- Recommendation systems which suggest songs & artists based on previous listening, friends' activities, as well as coummunity trends.
- Recommendations could be recieved via analysis of song metadata analysing musical attributes.

From Curators
- A curator is someone who knows lots about music playing or suggesting it for other who perhaps don't know as much." They include journalists, DJs or even regular users who are knowledgeable about music.
- Recommedations could be recieved via curated and user created playlists.
By Serendiptity
- Unplanned accidental discovery though physical and digital mediums.
- Recommendations could be recieved via song discovered through Shazam, or chancing upon a new artist or stumbling upon a playlist that you fell in love with.

Design directions

Keeping in mind the results of the market study and user interviews, design directions were laid out.

Celebrate music discovery
Discovery centered application - give the user multiple ways to discover new music.

Social Activity
Show the user what his friends are listening to let him show his friends what he's listening.

Simple & Fluid philosophy
Do away with nested hierarchies and information and visual clutter.


Rich visuals
Retain the visual richness of the original app, but bring it up speed with prevelant trends.
Ideation & approach

After imbibing the findings from user interviews and market study design philosophy for the redesign was pretty simple. I established an Active Discover > Listen > Share approach for the app..
