HP - Sales Compensation Solution

Building the platform for Salesperson

Role: Interaction Designer Team: Project Manager | Designer | UX Architect | 2 engineers Client: Hewlett-Packard (HP) Device: Web Portal Duration: 11 Month | 2017-2017

Project Overview
Context

The redesign of Sales Compensation Solution for HP, to solved many problems that had manifested over and over the years. It aimed to modernize solution by bringing MS technology and HP new design system. Their current sales Compensation Solution was one of the first products to adopt the new framework and helped influence its future direction.

Problem Statement

HP wanted to replace its Sales Compensation solution. The existing solution was 22 years old, and had leaking sales credit, had long lead time to process credits and needed abundant resources to run.

Solution

Modernize the solution by changing the technology and design, also simplifying the process.

Design System
Widgets Design (Sales Compensation tool)

Creating a successful sales compensation program in today’s changing business environment was a challenging task. The main idea was to create a simple and easy to grasp widgets in the UI.

Credit: The credit component was one of the most important part of the solution, which used to tell the sales employees about the credit history. I used the graph which also tells the overall status of the credit.

Deal tracking: Deal tracking widget was created to tell the current status of a particular deal. Where I used the data tables to show the modular layout, to add/ remove/ manage varied Content.

Notification: I used notifications widget to tell the users about the source of the information as well as for generic notifications. Also it had expandable content.




Dashboard and Reporting

The dashboard was created to support immediate decision-making. The idea was to present the complex information in a way that is easy to interpret, alert users to problems, and make the next action obvious. In addition I showed the key success metrics and showed the detailed data about the action “on the ground.”
