Shadow IT service

Designing for employees and IT team

Role: Lead Designer Team: Product Manager | UX Researcher | Dev Architect | Engineers Client: Contoso Device: Web Portal Duration 7 Month | 2019 - 2020

Project Overview
Context

We build a Shadow IT service which is part of digital risk platform for our customer CONTOSO. That gives all the information about the IT risks, assessment, planning and monitoring through a single platform.

Problem Statement

Employees/Users sometimes are so engrossed in their work or finishing up a specific task that unknowingly they might download an unauthorized software or app and as a result they invite an unwanted threat to them and their organization. It could be a virus, a data breach, or other cyber threats that could attack a well-functioning team, organization or individual.

Solution

The solution is a based on IT team's attentiveness. So that the IT team can get to know what kind of unauthorised tools or files are downloaded by the employees and how can they make it more secure by accessing the threat and help employees make their work easier and faster.

The solution will allow employees to experiment with new tools while mitigating Shadow IT risks and provide a competitive advantage.

Process
Understand the space

To clarify existing shortcomings, I needed to better understand entire ecosystem of risk management platform, what is the existing behavour of the user while using current system, how they do assessment and implement the controls. I did this through:

1. Domain Study - Shadow IT process

Employees/Users sometimes are so engrossed in their work or finishing up a specific task that unknowingly they might download an unauthorized software or app and as a result they invite an unwanted threat to them and their organization. It could be a virus, a data breach, or other cyber threats that could attack a well-functioning team, organization or individual.

2. Understanding customer existing system

To empathise users and understanding their existing pain points, we did virtual workshop with users from different profile groups, asked them to perform some predefined tasks and we were noted down the obeservations.

  • Key insights:
  • - Overwhelming data - User specific data is desired
  • - For employee, its very hard to understand & identify their incident which has converted into a problem.
  • - The information hierarchy is not so good. Everything looks equally important.
  • - The flows are very confusing.
  • - There are multiple ways to perform a task. But, none of them are proving a good experience.
3. User surveys analysis

Stakeholder did survey of government and corporate office workers in Boston and Washington, D.C. to gather anonymous selfreports of work-related security behaviors and attitudes.

One insight we learned was that as per study reported that 35 percent of employees feel like they need to work around their company's security policies just to get their job done. Which encouraged me to continue thinking of rest 65% employee, how we are empowering them to use their preferred tools can deliver long-term benefits with the help of shadow IT under company security policies.

4. App analysis

​I played with many existing homework (or related) apps from the Internet to learn from what's already been done. To know and understand the strengths and weaknesses of our competition, I did heuristic evalution 5 topmost apps. Also look at some patterns that shaped my design thinking included use of color for class hierarchy and typography to emphasize the most crucial information, the assignment due date.

Key Insights

During the synthesis of our findings, I recognized some common pain paints users experienced when they are using risk platform.

User Insights
  • - Difficult to navigate
  • - Forgetfulness. People get busy.
  • - Too complex to understand the content
  • - Communicate and collaborate.
  • - Educate and train
  • - Streamline governance
Business thoughts about existing system

Current system is driven by Oracle platform in relation to UX/UI. Application has its own constrains, but the business is looking in to how this solution is solving the userbase’s problem for their viber audience.

  • Key requests - How might we
  • - Make the current look and usability to more intutive in terms of interaction
  • - Provide users required information and get the sense of their problem’s current status
  • - Help business role succeed - to have a quick view on the overall problem’s and be able to identify the section of the challenges/problems asking.
  • - Help users to get proper timeline view of when the problem is created and resloved.
Personas
Based on the interview results, I designed three personas who can get most benefits from the system.
MARC HAYES
36 years | Business Owner

“I am busy with lots of things within my Business line and being the owner of an IT application is far from my top priorities. I do like to use cool apps like Slack and Survey Monkey to deliver on my business goals – they are fast, reliable, easy! Our IT department not always have the latest software, I don’t have time to wait for their help”

Needs
- Be able to find and onboard new IT applications quickly
- To be able to perform tasks quickly
- Status should be clear and act quickly

Pain Points
- Don’t want applications to be blocked.
- I don’t want to run into trouble of my applications being hacked and confidential data being exposed online.
- Complex online processes

SAM
28 years | IT Manager

“I manage the portfolio of IT services for my specific business. I like IT and the cool stuff we can do with it; With all these new apps we can work 10 times as efficient! Hey have you hear about this new app called Production Tiger?..

Needs
- To be able to find information quickly and easily
- Having the right information to hand when required
- I might need to delegate my responsibilities to my team, as sometimes I really don’t have any time for Service IT

Pain Points
- I don’t have much time, so I need to be able to decide quickly.
- Too much irrelevant information on pages.
- Long-term I want to see the value of how my behaviour's around BMIT help my company to succeed.

ADAM
45 years | QA Analyst

I work with Company in the Risk Management team. I am responsible for verification of risk assessments and control implementation by BO

Needs
- Having the right information to hand when required
- See the value of how my behavior's around BMIT help my company to succeed.
- Validate if controls were properly implemented by business owner in case of control review

Pain Points
- I don’t have much time, so I need to be able to decide quickly.
- The quality of completed risk assessments are inspected and can challenged.
- Too much irrelevant information on pages
